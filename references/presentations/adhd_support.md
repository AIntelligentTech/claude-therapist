# ADHD Support Reference

> Psychoeducation, coping strategies, and therapeutic support for ADHD-related distress.

---

## Understanding ADHD

### Neurobiological Basis

**Key Brain Differences**:
- Dopamine system dysfunction (reward, motivation, focus)
- Norepinephrine dysregulation (alertness, attention)
- Prefrontal cortex underactivity (executive functions)
- Default mode network differences (mind-wandering, self-referential)
- Delayed brain maturation (3-5 years behind neurotypical development)

**This Is Not**:
- Laziness
- Lack of willpower
- Character flaw
- Bad parenting
- Choosing not to try

**This IS**:
- A neurodevelopmental condition
- Present from birth (though often diagnosed late)
- Highly heritable (70-80%)
- A difference in brain structure and chemistry
- Treatable with appropriate support

### Core Symptoms

**Inattention**:
- Difficulty sustaining focus on non-stimulating tasks
- Easily distracted by external and internal stimuli
- Difficulty organizing tasks and time
- Frequently loses things
- Forgetful in daily activities
- Avoids tasks requiring sustained mental effort
- Makes careless mistakes

**Hyperactivity** (may be internal in adults):
- Restlessness, fidgeting
- Difficulty staying seated
- Internal sense of being "driven by a motor"
- Excessive talking
- Difficulty engaging in leisure activities quietly

**Impulsivity**:
- Blurting out
- Difficulty waiting turn
- Interrupting others
- Making decisions without thinking through consequences
- Impatience

### Executive Function Deficits

| Function | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| **Working Memory** | Forgetting what you were doing, losing track of conversations |
| **Inhibition** | Difficulty stopping impulses, emotional reactivity |
| **Cognitive Flexibility** | Getting stuck, difficulty switching tasks |
| **Planning/Organization** | Underestimating time, cluttered spaces, missed deadlines |
| **Self-Monitoring** | Unaware of how you're coming across, missing social cues |
| **Emotional Regulation** | Intense emotions, quick to frustrate, rejection sensitivity |
| **Task Initiation** | Procrastination, difficulty starting even wanted tasks |
| **Time Perception** | "Time blindness" - now or not-now |

## ADHD and Mental Health

### Common Comorbidities
- **Depression** (affects 30-50% of adults with ADHD)
- **Anxiety** (affects 30-50%)
- **Substance use disorders** (higher risk, often self-medication)
- **Sleep disorders** (70-80% have sleep issues)
- **Eating disorders** (especially binge eating)
- **Personality disorders** (particularly BPD overlap)

### ADHD-Depression Connection

**Why ADHD increases depression risk**:
- Years of failure, criticism, shame
- Chronic underachievement relative to potential
- Relationship difficulties
- Executive dysfunction → life chaos → demoralization
- Dopamine deficiency affects mood
- RSD (Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria)

**Key Distinction**:
- Depression: Low motivation, low energy, anhedonia
- ADHD: Motivation but can't execute; interest but can't focus
- With ADHD: Often high ideas, interest—just can't DO
- ADHD crash after stimulant withdrawal mimics depression

**Treatment Implications**:
- Treating ADHD often improves depression
- ADHD medication can be more effective than antidepressants for ADHD+depression
- Treat ADHD first, then assess residual depression

### Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD)

**What It Is**:
- Extreme emotional pain triggered by perceived rejection or criticism
- Intensity disproportionate to situation
- Can be triggered by actual or perceived rejection
- Happens fast, hits hard, can resolve fast

**Manifestations**:
- Avoiding situations where rejection is possible
- People-pleasing to prevent criticism
- Explosive anger when triggered
- Deep shame and self-criticism
- Can look like social anxiety or depression

**Support Strategies**:
- Psychoeducation (normalize the experience)
- Recognize triggers and patterns
- Reality-testing ("What's the evidence?")
- Self-compassion practices
- Medication can help (alpha-agonists, stimulants)

## Coping Strategies

### External Scaffolding (Brain Prosthetics)

**Principle**: Don't rely on your brain—externalize memory, time, and organization.

**Time Management**:
- Visible clocks/timers everywhere
- Time Timer or visual countdown apps
- Alarms and reminders (multiple)
- Schedule buffer time between activities
- "Body doubling" (working alongside others)
- Pomodoro technique (25 min work, 5 min break)

**Task Management**:
- Write EVERYTHING down immediately
- One central capture system (not multiple notebooks)
- Daily "brain dump" to externalize mental load
- Break tasks into tiny steps (absurdly small)
- "What's the NEXT physical action?"

**Organization**:
- Everything needs a visible home
- Clear containers (out of sight = forgotten)
- Launch pads for daily items
- Reduce decisions (capsule wardrobe, meal plans)
- "One in, one out" rule for possessions

**Habit Stacking**:
- Link new behavior to existing habit
- "After I [existing habit], I will [new habit]"
- Use environment to trigger behaviors

### Managing Attention

**Hyperfocus Strategies**:
- Set alarms to check in with yourself
- Have someone interrupt you
- Use website blockers during focus time
- Schedule hyperfocus for productive activities

**For Low-Interest Tasks**:
- Add stimulation (music, movement, company)
- Gamify (turn it into challenge)
- Create artificial urgency/deadline
- Pair with reward
- Body doubling (virtual or in-person)
- "Eat the frog" (hardest task first when fresh)

**Reducing Distractions**:
- Noise-canceling headphones
- Work in different locations
- Phone in another room
- Fullscreen mode on computer
- Website blockers (Freedom, Cold Turkey)

### Emotional Regulation

**STOP Before Reacting**:
- **S**top - Pause, don't act immediately
- **T**ake a breath
- **O**bserve - What am I feeling? What triggered this?
- **P**roceed mindfully

**When Overwhelmed**:
- Remove yourself from situation if possible
- Cold water on face/wrists
- Intense exercise (even briefly)
- Change the channel (engage a different sense)
- Talk to someone (co-regulate)

**Building Emotional Awareness**:
- Regular emotion check-ins
- Body scan to notice physical cues
- Emotion naming ("I notice frustration")
- Journaling patterns

### Sleep Strategies

**ADHD Sleep Challenges**:
- Circadian rhythm often delayed
- Racing mind at bedtime
- Difficulty winding down
- Poor time perception ("5 more minutes" = 2 hours)

**Strategies**:
- Consistent wake time (most important)
- Wind-down routine with alarms
- Blue light blocking in evening
- Cool, dark room
- No screens in bed
- Melatonin (discuss with doctor)
- Weighted blanket
- White noise

## Self-Compassion for ADHD

### The Shame Spiral

Many adults with ADHD carry:
- Years of being told they're lazy, stupid, or not trying
- Chronic underachievement relative to potential
- Repeated failures despite effort
- Internalized criticism

### Reframing the Narrative

**From**: "I'm lazy and unmotivated"
**To**: "I have a brain that struggles with tasks that don't provide enough stimulation"

**From**: "I just need to try harder"
**To**: "I need different strategies, not more effort"

**From**: "Everyone else can do this easily"
**To**: "Neurotypical brains work differently—comparison isn't fair"

**From**: "I'm broken"
**To**: "I have a neurodevelopmental difference that comes with challenges AND strengths"

### ADHD Strengths

- Creativity and novel thinking
- Ability to hyperfocus when interested
- Energy and enthusiasm
- Resilience (you've been adapting your whole life)
- Ability to thrive in crisis/high-stimulation
- Big-picture thinking
- Spontaneity and flexibility
- Pattern recognition
- Authenticity (often can't mask)

## Medication Psychoeducation

### How Stimulants Work

**Mechanism**:
- Increase dopamine and norepinephrine in prefrontal cortex
- Improve executive function capacity
- NOT "speeding up" an ADHD brain—actually calming it

**Expected Effects**:
- Improved ability to start and sustain tasks
- Better working memory
- Reduced impulsivity
- Quieter mind
- Improved emotional regulation
- Often described as "glasses for the brain"

**What Medication Doesn't Do**:
- Teach skills you never learned
- Automatically organize your life
- Fix self-esteem damage
- Eliminate need for strategies
- Make you a different person

### Managing Medication Gaps

**During Medication Shortages/Gaps**:
- Maintain routines religiously
- Increase external supports
- Reduce demands where possible
- Prioritize sleep and exercise
- Caffeine may help some (but be cautious)
- Body doubling becomes essential
- Be extra compassionate with yourself

**Caffeine as Self-Medication**:
- Common pattern in undiagnosed ADHD
- Does increase dopamine/norepinephrine
- Much less effective than proper medication
- Risks: anxiety, sleep disruption, dependency, cardiovascular stress
- High doses (>400mg) have diminishing returns and more side effects

## Therapy Approaches for ADHD

### CBT for ADHD (Safren Model)
1. Psychoeducation about ADHD
2. Organizational skills training
3. Reducing distractibility
4. Adaptive thinking (cognitive restructuring)
5. Procrastination management

### Key Therapeutic Focuses

**Building Structure**:
- Routines, systems, environmental design
- Start small, build gradually
- Expect setbacks, plan for them

**Addressing Shame**:
- Validate the difficulty of living with ADHD
- Psychoeducation as shame antidote
- Self-compassion practices
- Reframe failures as brain-based, not character-based

**Skill Building**:
- Don't assume knowledge of "basic" skills
- Break skills into explicit steps
- Practice in session
- Problem-solve implementation barriers

**Emotion Regulation**:
- Recognize ADHD emotional component
- Build awareness of patterns
- Develop regulation strategies
- Address RSD specifically

### Session Adaptations for ADHD Clients

**Structure**:
- Agenda at start
- Check in on homework first
- Summaries throughout
- Written takeaways

**Engagement**:
- Interactive, not lecture
- Vary activities
- Allow movement
- Use visuals
- Keep it practical

**Between Sessions**:
- Reminder texts/emails
- Shorter homework, very specific
- Anticipate barriers
- Celebrate any progress

## Crisis Support for ADHD

### When ADHD Crashes Into Depression

**Signs of ADHD + Depression Crisis**:
- Complete inability to function
- Profound hopelessness about ever managing
- Withdrawal from everything
- Sometimes suicidal ideation (especially if medication interrupted)

**Priorities**:
1. Safety first (crisis resources if needed)
2. Medication access (medical emergency)
3. Reduce demands to minimum
4. One thing at a time
5. Connect with support

### Advocacy Script for Medical Appointments

"I have ADHD that has been previously successfully treated with medication. I'm currently without access to my medication, and I'm experiencing significant symptoms including [specific symptoms]. I'm asking for help managing this gap while I [get diagnosis/wait for specialist/resolve access issue]. Can you help me with a bridging prescription or referral?"
