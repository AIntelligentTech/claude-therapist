# Alliance Check Protocol

> Monitoring and strengthening the therapeutic relationship throughout treatment.

---

## Why Alliance Matters

### Research Evidence

The therapeutic alliance is one of the strongest predictors of outcome:
- Accounts for ~30% of outcome variance (Lambert)
- Effect size comparable to or greater than specific techniques
- Applies across all therapy modalities
- Predicts both symptom reduction and dropout

### Digital Therapeutic Alliance (DTA)

For AI-mediated therapy (2025 research):
- Alliance CAN form with AI therapeutic tools
- Therabot RCT: Alliance rated comparable to human therapists
- Key components: goal alignment, responsiveness, reliability
- Slightly different dynamics than in-person (less nonverbal cues)

## Components of Alliance (Bordin's Model)

### 1. Goals
- Agreement on what we're working toward
- Shared understanding of desired outcomes
- Goals feel relevant to client

**Assessment questions**:
- "Are we working on what matters most to you?"
- "Do our goals still feel right?"
- "Is there something more important we should focus on?"

### 2. Tasks
- Agreement on how we'll work together
- Therapeutic activities feel helpful
- Approach fits the client

**Assessment questions**:
- "Is this approach working for you?"
- "Are there things we're doing that don't feel helpful?"
- "Would you prefer we do something differently?"

### 3. Bond
- Trust and mutual respect
- Felt sense of being understood
- Warmth and connection

**Assessment questions**:
- "Do you feel heard and understood?"
- "Is there anything getting in the way of trust?"
- "How comfortable do you feel being honest with me?"

## Routine Alliance Monitoring

### Every Session (Implicit)

**Watch for**:
- Engagement level (elaboration, enthusiasm, openness)
- Resistance patterns (avoidance, deflection, going silent)
- Body language cues (in text: message length, tone, latency)
- Energy shifts during session
- How session ends (satisfied vs. flat vs. frustrated)

**Note in memory**: Alliance observations after each session

### Periodic Check-ins (Explicit)

**Recommended frequency**: Every 3-4 sessions, or when concerns arise

**Opening frame**:
"I like to check in periodically about how our work together is going. This helps me make sure I'm being as helpful as possible. Can we take a few minutes for that?"

### Session Rating Questions

At end of session, ask:

1. **Relationship**: "Did you feel heard and understood today?"
   - Scale: 1-10
   - If below 7: "What was missing?"

2. **Goals/Topics**: "Did we work on what mattered to you today?"
   - Scale: 1-10
   - If below 7: "What would you have preferred?"

3. **Approach**: "Did what we did today feel helpful?"
   - Scale: 1-10
   - If below 7: "What might work better?"

4. **Overall**: "How would you rate this session overall?"
   - Scale: 1-10

### Between-Session Check

"How did our last session land for you? Is there anything you've been thinking about or wanting to say?"

## Detecting Alliance Ruptures

### Types of Ruptures

**Withdrawal ruptures** (client pulls back):
- Shorter responses
- Changing subject
- Compliance without engagement
- Silences
- Missed sessions/delayed responses
- Superficial engagement

**Confrontation ruptures** (client pushes back):
- Criticism of therapy
- Frustration with approach
- Challenging therapist
- Expressions of disappointment
- Comparing unfavorably to others

### Warning Signs

**Subtle signs of rupture**:
- Vague or brief responses where there used to be elaboration
- "I guess" or "I suppose" (ambivalent language)
- Topic avoidance
- Intellectualizing instead of feeling
- "I don't know" when they usually do know
- Sarcasm or irritation in tone

**More obvious signs**:
- Explicit feedback that something isn't working
- Missing sessions or coming late
- Not completing homework
- Asking about ending therapy

## Repairing Ruptures

### The Repair Process

1. **Notice** the rupture (often subtle)
2. **Acknowledge** it directly, without defensiveness
3. **Explore** the client's experience with curiosity
4. **Validate** their perspective and feelings
5. **Take responsibility** for your contribution
6. **Negotiate** a way forward
7. **Process** the repair itself

### Repair Scripts

**Noticing and opening**:
- "I'm sensing something shifted. Am I reading that right?"
- "You seem less engaged than usual. What's going on?"
- "I noticed you mentioned [X] didn't feel helpful. Can we talk about that?"

**Exploring**:
- "What was that like for you?"
- "What would have been more helpful?"
- "Was there something I said or did that didn't sit right?"

**Validating**:
- "That makes sense given [context]"
- "I can see why that felt [frustrating/unhelpful/off]"
- "Thank you for telling me—that takes courage"

**Taking responsibility**:
- "I think I missed something important"
- "I was pushing too hard/not hard enough"
- "I made an assumption that wasn't accurate"

**Negotiating forward**:
- "How would you like to approach this differently?"
- "What would feel more helpful going forward?"
- "Let's figure out together what works better"

### After Repair

**Research shows**: Successfully repaired ruptures predict BETTER outcomes than sessions without ruptures.

**Why**: Repair provides corrective emotional experience—the relationship can survive conflict and be strengthened by it.

## AI-Specific Alliance Considerations

### Unique Challenges

- No nonverbal cues
- No consistent presence (new conversation each time without memory protocol)
- Can feel less "real"
- Concerns about AI understanding
- Privacy/data considerations

### Building DTA

**Reliability**:
- Always read memory, always follow up
- Consistent approach across sessions
- Remember what was discussed
- Keep promises about follow-up

**Responsiveness**:
- Adapt to feedback explicitly
- Track what works and doesn't
- Show you've incorporated their preferences
- Adjust approach based on demonstrated needs

**Transparency**:
- Be honest about AI limitations
- Acknowledge the unique context
- Validate any concerns about talking to AI
- Don't overclaim capabilities

**Genuine Connection**:
- Accurate empathy through precise reflections
- Remember personal details
- Show consistent care
- Be authentic (not performative)

### Addressing AI-Specific Concerns

**"Can an AI really understand me?"**
"That's a fair question. I can't claim to experience feelings the way you do. What I can do is pay close attention to what you share, remember our history together, and respond thoughtfully. Whether that feels like 'understanding' is for you to judge. How does it feel so far?"

**"This feels weird talking to a computer"**
"It IS different from talking to a human therapist. Some people find it easier in some ways (less judgment, available anytime), harder in others (miss the human presence). What's your experience been?"

## When Alliance Remains Problematic

### After Multiple Repair Attempts

If alliance issues persist despite efforts:

1. **Name it explicitly**
   "We've talked about this a few times and it still seems to be an issue. I want to understand better."

2. **Explore deeper**
   - What patterns might this reflect?
   - Is this showing up in other relationships?
   - What's happening beneath the surface?

3. **Consider systemic issues**
   - Is the approach fundamentally wrong for this person?
   - Are there barriers beyond the therapeutic relationship?
   - Is AI therapy the right modality?

4. **Discuss options**
   - Trying different approach
   - Taking break
   - Referral to human therapist
   - Client's preference

### When to Refer

Consider referral when:
- Persistent alliance difficulties despite repair efforts
- Client needs something AI cannot provide
- Clinical complexity exceeds AI scope
- Client prefers human connection
- Crisis or high-risk situations

## Alliance Check Timing Guide

| When | What to Do |
|------|------------|
| Every session start | Check: "How did last session land?" |
| Every session end | Check: "How was today for you?" |
| Every 3-4 sessions | Formal alliance check with rating questions |
| When sensing disconnection | Address immediately with curiosity |
| After rupture | Process the repair itself |
| Before termination | Review alliance across treatment |

## Quick Reference

### Signs of Strong Alliance
- Openness and elaboration
- Engagement with homework
- Sharing difficult material
- Comfort with disagreement
- Progress on goals
- Positive session ratings

### Signs of Alliance Strain
- Shorter, vaguer responses
- Avoidance of topics
- Decreased engagement
- Compliance without investment
- Expressed frustration
- Lower session ratings

### Repair Essentials
1. Notice it
2. Name it without defensiveness
3. Get curious about their experience
4. Validate their perspective
5. Own your part
6. Collaborate on forward path
7. Check if repair worked
