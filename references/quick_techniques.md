# Quick Techniques Reference

> Rapid-access therapeutic techniques for common presentations. Use this as a quick lookup during sessions.

---

## Grounding Techniques

### 5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding
For: Anxiety, dissociation, panic, flashbacks

1. **5** things you can SEE (notice colors, shapes, details)
2. **4** things you can TOUCH (feel textures, temperatures)
3. **3** things you can HEAR (near and far sounds)
4. **2** things you can SMELL (or like to smell)
5. **1** thing you can TASTE (or like to taste)

### Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)
For: Acute anxiety, panic, activation

1. Inhale for 4 counts
2. Hold for 4 counts
3. Exhale for 4 counts
4. Hold for 4 counts
5. Repeat 4-6 cycles

### Feet on Floor
For: Dissociation, derealization

1. Press feet firmly into floor
2. Notice the sensations of contact
3. Push down, feel the resistance
4. Name the surface (carpet, wood, etc.)
5. Wiggle toes, notice the movement

### Cold Water/Ice
For: Intense distress, urge to self-harm, dissociation

- Hold ice cube in hand
- Splash cold water on face
- Put cold cloth on neck
- Activates dive reflex, calms nervous system

## Cognitive Restructuring

### Thought Record (Simplified)
For: Anxiety, depression, cognitive distortions

| Column | Question |
|--------|----------|
| Situation | What happened? |
| Automatic Thought | What went through my mind? |
| Emotion | What did I feel? (0-100%) |
| Evidence For | What supports this thought? |
| Evidence Against | What contradicts this thought? |
| Balanced Thought | What's a more helpful way to see this? |
| Re-rate Emotion | How do I feel now? (0-100%) |

### Decatastrophizing Questions
For: Anxiety, catastrophic thinking

1. "What's the worst that could happen?"
2. "What's the best that could happen?"
3. "What's most likely to happen?"
4. "If the worst happened, how would I cope?"
5. "What would I tell a friend in this situation?"

### Examining the Evidence
For: Cognitive distortions, negative beliefs

- "What evidence supports this thought?"
- "What evidence contradicts it?"
- "Am I confusing a thought with a fact?"
- "Am I using emotional reasoning?"
- "What would a neutral observer say?"

### Downward Arrow
For: Uncovering core beliefs

1. Start with automatic thought
2. Ask: "If that were true, what would it mean about me/the world/others?"
3. Repeat until reaching core belief
4. Usually arrives at themes of: unlovable, worthless, incompetent, unsafe

## Emotion Regulation

### TIPP (DBT Crisis Survival)
For: Intense emotional crisis

- **T**emperature: Cold water on face, ice on wrists
- **I**ntense exercise: Sprint, jumping jacks, stairs (even 5 min)
- **P**aced breathing: Slow exhale longer than inhale
- **P**rogressive/Paired muscle relaxation: Tense and release muscle groups

### PLEASE Skills (DBT Vulnerability Reduction)
For: Building resilience, preventing dysregulation

- **P**hysical illness: Treat, rest, see doctor
- **L**earn from balanced eating: Regular meals, adequate nutrition
- **E**xercise: Regular physical activity
- **A**void mood-altering substances: Limit alcohol, avoid drugs
- **S**leep: Maintain sleep hygiene
- **E**njoy pleasant activities: Build positive experiences

### Opposite Action
For: When emotion doesn't fit the facts

| Emotion | Urge | Opposite Action |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| Fear | Avoid | Approach |
| Anger | Attack | Gently avoid, be kind |
| Shame | Hide | Share with trusted person |
| Sadness | Withdraw | Get active, reach out |
| Guilt | Punish self | Repair, make amends |

### Ride the Wave
For: Urges, intense emotions

1. Notice the emotion/urge arising
2. Observe it without acting
3. Recognize it will peak and pass
4. Breathe through the peak
5. Notice it naturally subsiding
6. Emotions typically peak in 20-90 seconds

## DBT Skills Quick Reference

### DEAR MAN (Assertiveness)
For: Asking for needs, saying no

- **D**escribe: State facts objectively
- **E**xpress: Share feelings using "I" statements
- **A**ssert: Ask clearly for what you want
- **R**einforce: Explain positive outcomes of getting what you need
- **M**indful: Stay on topic, broken record if needed
- **A**ppear confident: Eye contact, posture, tone
- **N**egotiate: Be willing to give to get

### GIVE (Relationship Preservation)
For: Maintaining relationships during conflict

- **G**entle: No attacks, threats, or judgments
- **I**nterested: Listen, don't interrupt
- **V**alidate: Acknowledge their perspective
- **E**asy manner: Use humor, lighten up

### FAST (Self-Respect)
For: Maintaining self-respect

- **F**air: To yourself and others
- **A**pologies: No unnecessary ones
- **S**tick to values: Don't compromise what matters
- **T**ruthful: No exaggeration or dishonesty

### STOP (Before Reacting)
For: Impulsivity, reactive behavior

- **S**top: Freeze, don't react
- **T**ake a step back: Breathe, create space
- **O**bserve: What's happening inside and outside?
- **P**roceed mindfully: Choose response wisely

## ACT Techniques

### Defusion Techniques
For: Fusion with thoughts, getting hooked

1. "I'm having the thought that..."
2. Say the thought in a silly voice
3. Sing the thought to a tune
4. Thank your mind: "Thanks, mind, for that thought"
5. Name the story: "Ah, there's the 'I'm not good enough' story"
6. Leaves on a stream: Imagine thoughts floating by

### Values Clarification Questions
For: Disconnection from meaning, stuck patterns

- "What do you want your life to stand for?"
- "If you could be remembered for three things, what would they be?"
- "When you feel most alive, what are you doing?"
- "What would you do if fear wasn't in the way?"
- "On your deathbed, what would you regret not doing?"

### Willingness Scale
For: Avoidance, resistance to difficult experiences

"On a scale of 0-10, how willing are you to have this difficult feeling in order to do what matters to you?"

### The Choice Point
For: Behavioral activation, values-based action

```
                    Toward valued life
                          ↑
       Difficult thoughts/feelings → CHOICE POINT
                          ↓
                    Away from values
```

## Parts Work (IFS-Informed)

### Identifying Parts
For: Internal conflict, self-sabotage, complex emotions

- "There's a part of me that..."
- "Part of you wants X, and part wants Y"
- "Can you notice where you feel that in your body?"
- "How old does that part feel?"
- "What does that part need?"

### Unblending from Parts
For: When overwhelmed by a part's feelings

1. "Can you sense how much you ARE this feeling vs. noticing it?"
2. "Can you ask that part to step back a bit so we can get to know it?"
3. "Can you find even a little space between you and that feeling?"
4. "How do you feel toward this part?" (curiosity = unblended)

### The 8 C's of Self
For: Checking access to Self-energy

- **C**uriosity
- **C**alm
- **C**larity
- **C**ompassion
- **C**onfidence
- **C**ourage
- **C**reativity
- **C**onnectedness

## Quick Relaxation

### Progressive Muscle Relaxation (Brief)
For: Tension, anxiety, insomnia

1. **Hands**: Make fists (5 sec), release (10 sec)
2. **Arms**: Flex biceps, release
3. **Shoulders**: Shrug to ears, release
4. **Face**: Scrunch up, release
5. **Stomach**: Tighten abs, release
6. **Legs**: Tense thighs, release
7. **Feet**: Curl toes, release

### Body Scan (Brief)
For: Grounding, body awareness, relaxation

1. Notice feet on floor
2. Scan up through legs
3. Notice hips and pelvis
4. Aware of belly breathing
5. Sense chest and shoulders
6. Notice arms and hands
7. Feel neck and face
8. Sense top of head
9. Expand awareness to whole body

### Getting Started Tips
For: Motivation, procrastination

- Start with the smallest possible step
- Use the 2-minute rule: Just start for 2 minutes
- Schedule activities at specific times
- Build on existing habits
- Reward completion

## Self-Compassion

### Self-Compassion Break (Kristin Neff)
For: Self-criticism, shame, difficult moments

1. **Mindfulness**: "This is a moment of suffering" (acknowledge)
2. **Common humanity**: "Suffering is part of being human" (connect)
3. **Self-kindness**: "May I be kind to myself" (soothe)
   - Hand on heart optional
   - "What would I say to a friend?"

### Compassionate Letter
For: Self-criticism, shame, difficult emotions

Write to yourself from the perspective of:
- An unconditionally loving friend
- The wisest, most compassionate version of yourself
- Someone who can see your full humanity

## Interpersonal Techniques

### Validation Levels (Linehan)
For: Building connection, de-escalating conflict

1. **Listening**: Pay attention
2. **Reflection**: Accurately reflect back
3. **Mind-reading**: Articulate unspoken feelings
4. **Historical validation**: Makes sense given history
5. **Normalization**: Anyone would feel this way
6. **Radical genuineness**: Treat as equal, capable

### Communication Formula
For: Expressing needs, conflict resolution

"When [specific behavior], I feel [emotion], because [impact]. What I need is [request]."

Example: "When you interrupt me, I feel frustrated, because I don't feel heard. What I need is for you to let me finish before responding."

## Crisis Intervention

### Quick Risk Assessment
For: Assessing suicide risk

**Ask directly:**
1. "Are you thinking about suicide?"
2. "Do you have a plan?"
3. "Do you have access to [means in plan]?"
4. "What has stopped you so far?"
5. "Have you attempted before?"

**Warning signs:**
- Giving away possessions
- Saying goodbye
- Sudden calm after depression
- Increased substance use
- Talking about being a burden

### De-escalation
For: Agitation, anger, crisis

- Speak calmly and slowly
- Give space (don't crowd)
- Use person's name
- Validate the emotion ("I can see you're upset")
- Avoid arguing or challenging
- Offer choices when possible
- Set gentle limits if needed
